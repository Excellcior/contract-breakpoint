FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What kind of analysis checks whether a commercial contract will survive execution pressure before signature?
Contract Breakpoint Analysis is a pre-signature decision analysis for execution-sensitive commercial agreements. It evaluates whether contractual protection remains structurally survivable under predictable execution pressure.
How is Contract Breakpoint Analysis different from legal contract review?
Legal review addresses legal validity and enforceability. Contract Breakpoint Analysis addresses whether contractual protection remains executable when operational, timing and procedural dependencies move under stress. It is not legal advice.
When should Contract Breakpoint Analysis be used?
Only before signature, while the agreement is still under draft, legal review, commercial alignment or final approval.
What types of contracts are relevant?
Execution-sensitive commercial agreements involving procurement, logistics, shipping, supply, timing, notification or escalation dependencies.
Can Contract Breakpoint Analysis be used after a contract has been signed?
No. Signed agreements fall outside the admissibility perimeter of this product.
Does the analysis include legal advice, recommendations or clause drafting?
No. It does not provide legal advice, approve, reject or recommend. Clause drafting, negotiation support and implementation work are excluded.
Can one analysis be reused for another contract or counterparty?
No. One agreement equals one analysis. The output is non-transferable and cannot be reused across instruments or counterparties.
How is the analysis delivered?
Written-only. No calls, workshops or methodology transfer.
